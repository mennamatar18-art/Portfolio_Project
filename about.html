About Alan Turing

Who Was Alan Turing ?

  • He was a British mathematician, computer scientist, logician, and cryptanalyst.

  • He is widely recognized as the Father of Modern Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence.

Birth

  • Born on June 23, 1912.
  • Born in Maida Vale, London, England.
  • Full Name: Alan Mathison Turing.

Education

  • 1922: Started his education at St. Michael's Primary School.
  • 1926: Continued his studies at Hazelhurst Preparatory School.
  • 1928: Attended Sherborne School and developed a strong interest in Mathematics.
  • 1931: Studied Mathematics at King's College,University of Cambridge.
  • 1936: Completed his PhD in Mathematics at Princeton University.

Career

  • 1939: Worked at Bletchley Park during World War II.
  • 1940-1941: Helped break the German Enigma code.
  • 1945: Contributed to the development of early computers.

Achievements

  • 1936:Introduced the concept of the Turing Machine.
  • 1950:Proposed the Turing Test.
  • Known as the Father of Computer Science.

Death

  • Died on June 7, 1954.
  • Passed away at the age of 41.
  • His contributions continue to inspire the world.

Legacy

  • Inspired modern computer science.
  • His work shaped Artificial Intelligence.
  • Remembered as one of the greatest scientists in history.
